# Patent pledge (defensive use)

This document describes LAM’s patent posture for an open protocol.

**Not legal advice.** If you need legal certainty, consult your counsel.

## Why this exists

LAM is designed to spread like a standard: **open protocol + conformance tests**.

If patent applications exist around LAM, the goal is to use them **defensively** (to prevent someone else from locking the ecosystem), not as a tollbooth.

## The pledge (plain English)

Subject to the terms below, **Tucker Olmstead will not assert** any patent rights owned or controlled by Tucker Olmstead (now or in the future) against you for:
- making, using, selling, offering for sale, importing, or distributing an implementation of the **LAM protocol**, or
- running the LAM Conformance Suite to verify compatibility,

**except** in the case of **defensive action**.

## What “defensive action” means

Patents may be asserted only if you (or your affiliates) initiate or materially participate in a patent claim/lawsuit against:
- Tucker Olmstead, or
- any user of LAM, **because** they implement or use the LAM protocol.

In other words: the patent posture is **non‑aggressive by default**, and becomes aggressive only in response to patent aggression.

## Scope of what’s covered

This pledge is intended to cover patent claims that are **necessarily infringed** by implementing the normative (“MUST”) behaviors of the LAM protocol for a given version, as defined by:
- `conformance/spec.md`

This pledge is not intended to cover unrelated product features, proprietary extensions, or uses that are not about implementing the LAM protocol.

## Trademarks are separate

This patent pledge does **not** grant any right to use the LAM trademarks.

For conformance-based name use (“LAM”, “LAM Certified”, “LAM Compatible”), see:
- `TRADEMARK_POLICY.md`

## No warranty

This pledge:
- does not grant rights to patents owned by third parties,
- does not provide any warranty of non‑infringement,
- and does not change the terms of the open-source licenses for the LAM code/docs.
